Privacy Policy

Last Updated: April 29, 2025

Introduction

Abundant Science is building rapid testing as a category from Canada to the world. Based in Toronto, Ontario, we are committed to making health testing more accessible while respecting and protecting your privacy.

This policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information. As we currently focus on gathering email sign-ups and biomarker interests, this policy is designed to address our current practices while establishing a foundation for our future services.

We’ve studied Canada’s largest health data breach—the 2019 LifeLabs incident—and used it to inform our commitment to stronger safeguards. Learn more here.

1. For Any & Every Body

What information we collect:

  • Email addresses from waitlist sign-ups
  • Biomarkers or tests that interest you (e.g., HIV, PrEP, Testosterone)
  • Referral source (ads, partner links, campaigns)
  • Your interaction with our website (pages visited, behavior)

What we will do with your information:

  • Store it securely using industry-standard encryption
  • Use it to send requested resources (e.g., free HIV testing guides)
  • Update you on developments related to your interests
  • Analyze anonymized, aggregated data to improve our services
  • Delete it upon your request or when no longer needed

What we won’t do:

  • Sell your personal information to third parties
  • Share your health interests with advertisers
  • Use your info for unrelated purposes without consent
  • Disclose your identity to others

2. For Healthcare Providers & Researchers

What we will do:

  • Share anonymized insights to improve healthcare access
  • Explore provider partnerships to expand testing
  • Participate in research with proper safeguards

What we won’t do:

  • Share your identity without informed consent
  • Allow researchers to contact you without opt-in
  • Include your identity in research or publications
  • Give third-party access to raw identifiable data

3. For Regulators & Diagnostics Producers

What we will do:

  • Comply with all Canadian privacy laws, including PIPEDA
  • Follow provincial health data laws where applicable
  • Maintain records as required by regulators
  • Report adverse events or safety issues as needed
  • Update this policy to reflect regulation changes

What we won’t do:

  • Share your personal data with regulators unless required
  • Give diagnostics companies access without consent
  • Transfer data internationally without protections in place
  • Collect excessive data under the excuse of compliance

4. For Customers & App Users

What we will do:

  • Be transparent as our platform grows
  • Use strong technical, administrative, and physical safeguards
  • Give you control of your data:
    • Request a copy
    • Correct inaccuracies
    • Delete data on request
    • Manage communication preferences
  • Notify you of major policy updates

What we won’t do:

  • Expose your test results or interests to other users
  • Use dark patterns to get consent
  • Track your location/device beyond what’s necessary
  • Keep your health data after account deletion (unless legally required)

Special Considerations for Sensitive Health Information

We know health data deserves extra protection. For more on how we treat biomarker or HIV/PrEP testing data, visit our special privacy section.

Legal Basis for Processing

  • Your consent when submitting email or interests
  • Our legitimate interest in improving access to health testing
  • Compliance with laws related to health information

International Users

We are based in Canada, but we respect privacy globally. As we grow, we will:

  • Adapt to local laws where you live
  • Honor your data rights wherever you are
  • Use safeguards for cross-border data transfers

Changes to This Policy

  • We’ll email you when major changes occur
  • Previous versions will remain available
  • We’ll clearly explain what changed and why
  • You’ll have time to review before updates take effect
